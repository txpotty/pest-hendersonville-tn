Unusual Noises at Night or Morning

Hearing scratching, scurrying, thumping, or even vocalizations from your attic, walls, or crawl space during the quiet hours can be a definitive sign of nocturnal or crepuscular wildlife activity. Animals like raccoons, opossums, bats, or flying squirrels are often most active during these times. The type of sound can often provide clues about the animal. Light scratching might indicate mice or small rodents, while heavier thumping and movement could point to larger animals like raccoons or squirrels. If you hear these sounds, it's highly likely you have unwanted guests. These noises are not just a nuisance; they indicate that animals are actively using your home as shelter, potentially building nests, or accessing food sources within your property. Ignoring such noises allows the infestation to become more entrenched, making removal more complex and increasing the potential for significant damage. Our professional assessment can pinpoint the exact location and identity of the noise-making critters. We use specialized equipment to listen within walls and in other difficult-to-access areas, ensuring we accurately identify the animal causing the disturbance. This precision is crucial for choosing the most effective removal methods and addressing the root of the problem. The timing and nature of the sounds are also important indicators for our technicians, providing valuable insights into the habits and species of the wildlife present. Don't dismiss those strange noises; they are your home telling you it needs professional attention for wildlife control.

Droppings or Urine Stains

Finding animal droppings or discovering urine stains in or around your home is a clear and undeniable sign of wildlife presence. The appearance and location of droppings can often help identify the animal responsible. For instance, bat guano is typically dark, pellet-like, and often found in piles below roosting areas. Raccoon droppings are larger and can contain various undigested materials. Urine stains can create persistent odors and can even seep through ceilings or walls, causing visible damage. Animal waste carries bacteria, viruses, and parasites, posing significant health risks. Handling animal waste without proper protection can lead to exposure to diseases like histoplasmosis, particularly when dealing with bat guano. These droppings and stains also attract other pests and can make areas of your home unsanitary. Professional cleanup is often necessary to safely remove contaminated materials and disinfect the affected areas. Our team is equipped to handle the safe removal and disposal of animal waste, minimizing health risks and restoring the hygiene of your property. We understand the importance of thorough cleaning and sanitation after wildlife removal, as lingering waste can continue to pose health threats and attract other pests. Identifying the type of droppings also aids in confirming the species of wildlife present, further informing our removal strategy. Don't attempt to clean up significant amounts of animal waste yourself; contact the professionals to ensure it's done safely and effectively. The presence of droppings is a clear indication of an active infestation that requires immediate professional attention. Our cleanup services go beyond simple aesthetics, focusing on eliminating potential health hazards and ensuring a clean and safe living environment for your family.

Damaged Property or Structures

Wildlife can cause a wide range of damage to your home. This includes chewing through electrical wiring, tearing up insulation for nesting material, damaging rooftops or siding to gain entry, and even destroying ductwork in attics or crawl spaces. Squirrels gnawing on wiring can create fire hazards, while raccoons are notorious for their strength and ability to rip apart building materials. The extent and type of damage often provide clues about the animal responsible and its access points.
